After completing an MSc in Civil Engineering at KTH (2004), Andreas Andersson worked as a structural engineer at the Swedish Transport Administration (Banverket/Trafikverket), while pursuing postgraduate studies at KTH that led to a Licentiate degree (2009) and a PhD (2011).

Subsequent international research periods included Roughan & O'Donovan Innovative Solutions in Dublin (Long Life Bridges), Southwest Jiaotong University in Chengdu (RISEN), and the Smart Structures Technology Laboratory at UIUC.

Current work focuses on bridge and railway infrastructure dynamics, including:

- Railway bridge dynamics for high-speed trains
- Train-track-bridge interaction
- Dynamic soil-structure interaction
- Field measurements, instrumentation, and signal processing
- Model updating and nonlinear FE analysis
- Vibration mitigation with external damper systems
- Impact loading and fatigue assessment of bridge structures
